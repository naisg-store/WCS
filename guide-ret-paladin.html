Retribution Paladin: Patch 12.0.7 Guide

The Meta Context: Holy Fire in the Void

In World of Warcraft: Midnight, Retribution Paladins have emerged as one of the most consistent and reliable melee DPS specs. Patch 12.0.7 has further refined the "Templar" and "Herald of the Sun" Hero Talent paths, offering players a choice between heavy single-target burst and sustained radiant cleave. Retribution's strength lies in its incredible utility and ease of execution. While other specs might struggle with complex setups, Retribution delivers high-impact damage with a robust defensive kit that makes them invaluable in both Mythic+ and raid progression.

[Suggested Image: A cinematic shot of a Retribution Paladin unleashing a Hammer of Light, illuminating a dark Midnight dungeon]

Stat Analysis: The Balance of Radiance

For Patch 12.0.7, the stat priority for Retribution remains relatively stable, though your choice of Hero Talent can slightly influence the weights. Generally, you should aim for:

Strength > Haste > Mastery > Critical Strike > Versatility

Haste is your most important secondary stat as it reduces the cooldown of your primary Holy Power generators and your Global Cooldown. You want enough Haste to ensure your rotation feels fluid and your Radiant Glory windows are maximized. Mastery (Hand of Light) is a close second, as it increases all Holy damage dealt, which is the vast majority of your output. Critical Strike is valuable but often comes naturally from gear, while Versatility remains a solid but unexciting choice for pure throughput.

Rotation Nuance: Spending Wisely

The Retribution rotation in 12.0.7 revolves around efficient Holy Power management. Your goal is to never over-cap Holy Power while ensuring you always have enough for your big spenders during Execution Sentence or Final Reckoning windows.

A key nuance in the current patch is the interaction between Wake of Ashes and your Hero Talents. If you are playing as a Templar, your Wake of Ashes transforms into Hammer of Light, which deals massive damage and should be used as soon as possible. The "pro-tip" for 12.0.7 is to track your Art of War procs closely—resetting your Blade of Justice is the key to maintaining high Holy Power generation and maximizing your uptime on Final Verdict.

Utility & Survival: The Ultimate Support

Retribution Paladins are the kings of utility. Blessing of Protection, Blessing of Sacrifice, and Blessing of Freedom can completely negate mechanics that would kill other players. In Patch 12.0.7, proactive use of Divine Shield and Shield of Vengeance allows you to stay in the fight during high-damage events. Don't forget Lay on Hands—it's a literal life-saver that can turn a wipe into a kill. Your Intercession (combat res) is also a critical tool that every raid team relies on.
